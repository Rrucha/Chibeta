About Me


Hello! My name is Hao Zhu, and I am a Computer Science sophomore transfer student of the Chi Beta pledge class at the University at Buffalo. I transferred to UB this semester from The City College of New York because I felt that I needed to break out of the comfort of my home. I was raised in Brooklyn, NY, but I moved to Staten Island, NY two years ago. Some of my hobbies include digital art as well as listening to music. I hope to one day achieve my dream of becomming a software engineer.




What I Did

  • During the cutting phase of the contruction project, I learned to make use of different tools in the woodshop, like the table saw, band saw, wall saw, and chop saw. Coordination during this phase was crucial as well, and I improved my time management skills by working out timeslots with my pledge brothers to see who can go to the woodshop and who can stay at lab staffing.

  • During the assembling phase of the construction project, I learned the intricate details on how the pieces of wood are put together. Before this I never thought about what goes inside a structure to make it stable, and I learned that supports were put inside. During the painting phase, I learned that it takes multiple coats of paint for the intended shade of a color to show when it dries.

  • Currently, me and my pledge brothers are working together to bring this website to fruition. What you see currently is the result of our dedicated time and hard work.
